I'm a reformed offender, a social activist, a good cook, a story listener and also a storyteller. I may have committed felonies in the past, If I could go back to those years, I wouldn't choose to change a thing. I believe it was what moulded me to be who I am today.

If anyone is going asked me if I would fall again, I couldn't give you a definitive answer. I am fighting the same war every day, just like anyone else. If there is anything else but fall, I will choose to fall forward and continue to pick myself up and keep walking. With a more resilient personality with many scars of my past, to share the foolishness in me that nevertheless made me maybe stronger but definitely wiser.

There are no perfect humans, but there one thing definitive, we are all cultured by cultures. In order to stay clean, it is both the community, as well as individuals to create or stay in, a healthy cultured environment, a drug-free, positive, resilient circle or group of friends that provide support and a safe haven for them to seek support without prejudices. Cultures cultivate personality; influence and encourage actions. It's a double-edged sword and it is important for a social influencer to set examples by forming a community where trust, cohesiveness, and kinship are fostered.

There one saying which I live by and also adhere by, "if we don't bother to listen to stories, don't bother at all". Everyone has a unique story of their own; of their pain, struggles and also their recovery and joy, stereotyping is still a common problem amongst our society. If we can't listen enough, we can't speak. There is only one thing that changes a person, that's LOVE.

  • The Straits Times: Causes Week 2016
  • Date : 04/12/2016
  • Initiative : The LongerTable

What we are doing and how we define LongerTable. It's not about helping the "poor" or "needy". It's really to stand in the gap and trying to bridge the missing values of "family". A value that is currently missing in our society. Social stigmatism extended not just within the marginalized, the norm of life such as wealth inequality, academic performance in school among the young, generational gap, to the "disabled", had become abnormal in the sight of our current society. It’s not about who has a "disability". It’s us who didn’t see any disability in them! That is the crucial part of LongerTable, we see them as our family, and in a family, there is no such thing as inequality. Nobody in a family is placed on a higher pedestal. Love and respect removed any barriers to love each other unconditionally. And such love changes a life. #MondaysNight #BringingHomeToWhereItMatters #ReturningDignity #Inclusive #LongerTable #AKA #TokPanjang

  • First Charity Drive: Joy Drive - A Mission to Lift and Encourage
  • Date : 28/12/2015
  • Initiative : The LongerTable - Beyond Chinatown

Our very first LongerTable Charity Drive beyond Chinatown. Our Fiscal year end on Christmas and there was a surplus of SGD$362. We decided that we would want to bless someone with the surpluses from our funds. I happened to see an article about this petrol station that offered their space for MINDS to set up a carwash kiosk, to allow the intellectually disabled kids to run their business. We prepared hampers and requested car owners to pledge to offer $50 for each car washed as a gesture to encourage and to uplift the kids, who choose to pick up skill and live by the works of their hands. I posted on my facebook and was expecting 7 - 8 friends to respond to this small charity drive, but almost 21 friends responded with 25 vehicles turn up. We had a fun time singing and interacting with the kids working at the petrol kiosk at SPC 616 Telok Blangah Road.
